Global Potato Company

What is the Global Potato Company?

It’s no secret that people need food to survive. This is a fact that is known. But the problem is food doesn’t appear out of thin air. It needs to be grown. And who grows it? Farms. But these farms often can’t distribute these crops to a wide market, so they let bigger distributors do it for them. Our plan is to become such a distributor. Our plan is to become a stronghold of our own land holdings and partner farms, making our reach further. This way, valuable land holdings don’t go to waste since we work those, however our crops will be doubled thanks to partnered farms.

What are our plans to grow?

Our plan is simple: there are many land holdings available for little money that we can use to grow potatoes. We sell these to distributors or farmer’s markets and use these to buy more cheap land. As we buy more and more land, we will earn more and more money until eventually we can start relying less and less on other distributors and distribute potatoes ourselves. With more money, we can start buying bigger amounts of land, especially in third world countries like in Africa or Southeast Asia. The good thing about potatoes is that they grow anywhere you can find dirt. Becoming a big distributor, we can then start partnering with private farms to distribute crops for a subsidy. This will increase crop yield by a greater amount, and will require us to not need to hire as many workers (except for our personal land holdings). The goal is to build a large empire of land for growing potatoes, and that eventually, every country on Earth will have a settlement from the GPC.

But why?

Potatoes are a valuable crop, in that they are easily grown in many climates and are enjoyed by many people in various different dishes. Where the GPC will come out victorious compared to competitors is our outreach. Buying cheap land in third-world countries will allow us to grow many potatoes at a relatively lower cost. I see potential in this idea due to the fast growth a company as such could get from such a simple crop. What about the future? I think that we don’t have to just stop at potatoes. As we grow more and more land, slowly we can start growing other crops. If possible, even livestock. Such a variance will definitely boost the company and will truly make us into a food empire. Slowly by slowly, almost everyone will need to rely on the GPC for food. Our outreach will be immense. With our great influence on the food industry, we can also build GPC’s impact by funding development projects in third-world countries to make them more technologically advanced and modern. This will cause them to be reliant on us and will make us very influential in global politics and world influence. Soon, the Global Potato Company will be a name spoken about all across the world. A small idea, coming from buying cheap land to grow potatoes, can become an empire that influences the entire world. And that, my friends, is the true vision. But it all can start from a potato.

Where are possible places to get land?

Low-populated and undeveloped areas will have a lot of good, cheap land for potato growing. Areas in Africa and Central and South Asia will be very good due to climate and available land. If the tensions with Russia end, then areas in Siberia will be incredibly beneficial due to availability and cost. Ukraine will also probably be good (if the war ends) due to its land already being great for growing wheat. Areas in Central/South America and the Caribbean will also be great due to availability, though we don’t know yet about cost. Looking on Zillow, there are many people selling land for just a dollar in many U.S. states as well.

What would it cost?

It is hard to gauge the cost at this point. Buying land is one thing, but there are way more costs in getting people to work the land and grow the potatoes. Not to mention packing and shipping (which is why we want to partner with a bigger company in the start to help with that). Overall, workforce and machinery could be our biggest problem starting up, not to mention any fees we need to pay regarding our company and land-owning fees.

Why should one take interest in this idea?

I am not currently in a position where I am able to start a company, buy the land holdings, employ a workforce and secure deals. However, I think that this idea has value. It is a fun thing to discuss and imagine and plan for what such a company could do and be like, even if it cannot actually start as of now. A project of such scale will take a lot of time. What starts as just an idea we discuss could one day become a global empire. It is always fun to imagine what could be.
